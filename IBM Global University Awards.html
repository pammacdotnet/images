Nominee Technical Background and Interests


PhD Dissertation Title: Quantum_Quantum Circuits and Software_Influence and reasoning mechanisms associated to Quantum Computing for logical-mathematical learning

Description:
Research Questions Q1. Are the IBM Q computers ready for real world applications and teaching realms? Q2. Which qubit configurations are best (if applicable) for the proposed specific test algorithm (binary quaternary search)? Q3. Can error mitigation contribute to enhance the probability of obtaining the correct results? Q4. Do IBM Q computers deliver sustained correct results over time that can contribute to strengthening the consolidation of mathematical and methodological intuition in students? Q5. How easy it is, for children, to write, upload and run quantum algorithms in a remote quantum hardware? Q6. Can quantum algorithms be taught in K-12 (or higher education) schools? Are specific children groups (for instance, those with high intellectual abilities) more prepared than average kids to operate with this new hardware and computer programming style? Methodology For Q1 to Q4: Test all publicly available IBQ computers by running a specific quantum program (binary quaternary search) in a daily and sustained manner, receiving their corresponding outputs and storing them accordingly for analysis. For Q5 to Q6: Visit ordinary schools and special teaching groups (academies, extracurricular learning institutions, online universities, etc.) and offer ad hoc training sessions and practical meetings where students of all ages, ethnicities and intelectual capacities are introduced to the world of QC. Carry out surveys on the impact of the concepts learned. Analysis Strategy For Q1 to Q4: Simple statistics about each QC's up time, correct result, and contribution of error mitigation in specific quantum programs. For Q5 to Q6: Correction of students test and compilation of surveys. Impact of potential results, risks/challenges Deliver a map of the future of quantum programming and how it will permeate society, taking the surveyed kids, students, academic levels, and even isolated schools as a reference framework.

Does this research relate to social or racial justice? No

Primary Research Interest: Quantum Circuits and Software

Student evaluation:
Raquel has carried out a very remarkable job even during the rather small period of time she has been involved in her PhD. Although her research subject has to do more with the interplay of Quantum Computing and education, the student has started by meticulously examining the quality of the response of these novel equipment. This is and important aspect in education, because children and secondary school students are in the need of sustained trends and stable links between the supplied inputs (programs+data) and outputs (the results of quantum applications). For this important and pedagogical reason and for the time being, Raquel has thoroughly tested IBM's quantum hardware (almost all publicly available IBM computers) over the course of a few months. The test has consisted in the execution of ad hoc algorithm (also designed and programmed by Mrs. Pérez). A paper with the obtained results and more related to the research questions Q1-Q4, has already been submitted for publishing in the International Journal of Artificial Inteligence and Multimedia (IJIMAI). A draft version of this work is available at https://github.com/pammacdotnet/GFUNIR/raw/main/ibmijimai.pdf). Besides, a literature review and analysis on the state-of-the-art of Quantum games and Education + Quantum Computing has been carried out. Two more research papers on these subjects are currently in the pipeline. In the next years of the PhD time span, Raquel will try to endeavour and distil the motivations and limitations in students who aspire to start learning Quantum Computing. The student will also measure among K-12 scholars the potential tangible effects of designing simple Quantum algorithms in the math and science learning process.
